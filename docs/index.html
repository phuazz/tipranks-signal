The study, in brief

Each week the analyst panel is captured and frozen — never revised, never backfilled — and joined to a point-in-time index universe with delisted names retained. The design is pre-registered: universe, signals, adjustment, inference and the decision rule were fixed in writing before any result existed. The known failure modes have named guards:

Guard 1 — look-ahead

Vendor sites show only current, recomputed scores and drop dead names. Forward snapshots only: each weekly panel is frozen at capture. History is accrued, never reconstructed.

Guard 2 — fake alpha

Bullish ratings cluster on high-beta growth, so raw forward returns flatter the signal. The headline is drift-adjusted, style-neutralised alpha — the adjustment that collapsed 14 apparent leads to 1 in a sibling study.

Guard 3 — costs

Revision signals churn. Liquidity floors (price and dollar-volume), tiered one-way costs on every rebalance, and a cost sweep — a gross-only result is a curiosity, not a finding.

The frozen menu — five schemes, and nothing else is ever graded

  • S1a — rating-change bucket (primary). Week-on-week consensus-label improvements between frozen snapshots, counted only when the latest rating date falls inside the window — composition phantoms excluded. revisions, not levels
  • S1b — best-analyst target revisions. The target level raised since the prior snapshot; upside moves caused purely by price do not qualify. event bucket
  • S1c — Smart-Score decile. The vendor composite tested as-is, once — the vendor-claim test. levels baseline
  • S2 — residualised composite. Equal-weight z-composite of the revision, target and insider axes, style-neutralised at construction; weights never optimised. our reconstruction
  • S3 — gated revision. S1a AND price-not-falling AND no net insider selling — the value-trap gate as a scheme. confirmation
A signal is KEPT only if its style-neutral, drift-adjusted alpha survives net of tiered costs with a bootstrap CI excluding zero and a drift-matched random-entry null under BH-FDR (q = 0.10, N = 5), with sign stability across horizons. Verdicts are barred before 26 weekly captures; graduation additionally requires an economic-magnitude floor, a deflated Sharpe ratio, and a 13-week paper track. Ratchet, clock-start and no-promotion rules keep the multiple-testing budget honest.

Research map

The evidence the design leans on. Revisions beat levels; target upside is a biased level but a usable relative signal; corroboration and multiple-testing discipline do the rest.

Revisions beat levels

  • Womack (1996), Journal of Finance. Recommendation changes are followed by drift, asymmetrically — downgrade drift is larger and longer-lived.
  • Barber, Lehavy, McNichols & Trueman (2001), JF. Consensus-level portfolios earn gross abnormal returns that transaction costs erase.
  • Loh & Stulz (2011), RFS. Only ~12% of recommendation changes are influential — reputable analysts acting away from consensus.

Targets and discipline

  • Brav & Lehavy (2003), JF. Target-price revisions move prices — information events conditional on the rating.
  • Da & Schaumburg (2011), JFM. Target-implied upside predicts within sector, not across the market.
  • Dechow & You (2020), TAR. Roughly 16–18 cents per dollar of implied upside is realised — the optimism haircut.
  • Harvey, Liu & Zhu (2016), RFS; Bailey & López de Prado (2014). Multiple testing inflates discovery; FDR control and the deflated Sharpe ratio are the correction.
